Competitive Robotics (FRC)

The Idea:

  • Create a robust and efficient robot capable of fast scoring, reliable climbing, and precise object handling.
  • Integrate all mechanical, electrical, and programming systems under a coordinated design workflow.
  • Balance mechanical complexity with ease of maintenance and competition reliability.

How?

  • Directed the Design and Build subteam, delegating fabrication and mentoring newer members.
  • Designed a 3D-angled intake funnel in Onshape with a dual-servo retractable folding mechanism for efficient game piece capture and stowing.
  • Fabricated a CNC-cut polycarbonate hanging mechanism and developed a two-stage cascade pulley lift powered by dual Kraken X60 motors for rapid extension.

Results:

  • Achieved 95% intake reliability with smooth handoffs to the end effector.
  • Enabled the 115 lb robot to climb onto a swinging low bar in 2.5 seconds.
  • Delivered a 57" lift extension in 0.25 seconds, supporting a 6.7 kg payload with high structural rigidity and precise motion.

Planetary Gearbox

The Idea:

  • Design a miniature, high-efficiency gearbox to maximize torque while minimizing space.
  • Utilize planetary gear configuration for balanced load distribution and smooth motion.
  • Integrate with a 12V brushed DC motor for practical mechanical testing.

How?

  • Modeled in SolidWorks with custom gear geometry and 0.1 mm print tolerances for precision meshing.
  • Included 6 bearings, 3 planet gears, and 1 sun gear to achieve compact, stable operation.
  • Applied multi-material 3D printing and fastened components with 9 embedded screws for rigidity.

Results:

  • Achieved 5× torque increase and 5× speed reduction from direct motor output.
  • Delivered 1 mm of precise motion at a 10 cm lever arm with minimal backlash.
  • Demonstrated an efficient, durable prototype suitable for small-scale robotic applications.

Robotic Hand

The Idea:

  • Create a compact, lifelike robotic hand capable of mimicking human finger movement.
  • Explore tendon-driven actuation for natural motion and smooth flexibility.
  • Design a functional demonstration model built entirely within a weekend timeframe.

How?

  • Modeled all components in Onshape, refining finger geometry for strength and joint precision.
  • Integrated 6 microservos and an Arduino Uno R3 for five programmable hand motions.
  • Tested and iterated multiple elastic and tendon configurations for realistic finger motion and return tension.

Results:

  • Improved finger strength by 40% through CAD optimization and material tuning.
  • Achieved smooth, controllable actuation using a joystick interface.
  • Completed a fully functional prototype despite material limits, time constraints, and compact design challenges.

Micro Robot Dog

The Idea:

  • Build an ultra-small, affordable robot dog after finding existing models too expensive.
  • Overcome the power limitations of microservos by distributing load through clever mechanical design.
  • Use elastic support systems to assist servo torque and mimic muscle-like movement.

How?

  • Designed and prototyped a single robotic leg as a proof of concept.
  • Implemented dual diagonal servo actuation with elastic tension sharing the upward load.
  • Tested lifting performance with 3D-printed components and lightweight linkages for structural efficiency.

Results:

  • Prototype leg successfully lifted 500 g, proving feasibility at small scale.
  • Demonstrated that microservos can achieve realistic motion when aided by elastic support.
  • Project halted before full quadruped completion due to electronics cost and control complexity, but validated key design principles.

UWFE Chain Tensioners

The Idea:

  • Improve manufacturability and assembly efficiency using DFMA principles.
  • Optimize component simplicity, tolerances, and material choice for real-world fabrication.
  • Support drivetrain performance through precise chain tensioning.

How?

  • Investigated and benchmarked multiple catch can design configurations, analyzing wall thickness, materials, and manufacturing methods.
  • Applied DFMA in SolidWorks to reduce part count and improve thermal performance.
  • Designed two precision shims to sit between the motor mount and driveshaft, incorporating proper hole tolerances for waterjet cutting.

Results:

  • Achieved a 25% reduction in part count while improving cooling efficiency.
  • Delivered manufacturable, waterjet-ready shim designs with correct fit and alignment.
  • Gained hands-on experience applying DFMA and tolerance analysis to functional drivetrain components.

Levitating Hydro Generator

The Idea:

  • Create a high-efficiency hydro generator by eliminating bearing friction through magnetic levitation.
  • Use overengineering as exploration, testing the limits of unconventional generator design.
  • Incorporate a flywheel system to maintain smooth, consistent rotation during operation.

How?

  • Built the frame from 2020 aluminum extrusions for precise alignment and rigidity.
  • Integrated 60 N52 neodymium magnets for levitation and power generation.
  • Wound custom 20 AWG copper coils and coupled them with a high-inertia flywheel for steady energy output.

Results:

  • Generated a peak power output of 6 W with an overall efficiency of ~5%.
  • Found that magnetic interference between the levitation and generator systems caused major energy losses.
  • Despite inefficiency, the project successfully demonstrated magnetic levitation feasibility in a functional generator setup.

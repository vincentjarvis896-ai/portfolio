The Idea:

  • Create a robust and efficient robot capable of fast scoring, reliable climbing, and precise object handling.
  • Integrate all mechanical, electrical, and programming systems under a coordinated design workflow.
  • Balance mechanical complexity with ease of maintenance and competition reliability.

How?

  • Directed the Design and Build subteam, delegating fabrication and mentoring newer members.
  • Designed a 3D-angled intake funnel in Onshape with a dual-servo retractable folding mechanism for efficient game piece capture and stowing.
  • Fabricated a CNC-cut polycarbonate hanging mechanism and developed a two-stage cascade pulley lift powered by dual Kraken X60 motors for rapid extension.

Results:

  • Achieved 95% intake reliability with smooth handoffs to the end effector.
  • Enabled the 115 lb robot to climb onto a swinging low bar in 2.5 seconds.
  • Delivered a 57" lift extension in 0.25 seconds, supporting a 6.7 kg payload with high structural rigidity and precise motion.

Planetary Gearbox

Insert Exploded View

Insert Testing Setup

Insert Performance Graph

The Idea

Design a miniature, high-efficiency gearbox to maximize torque while minimizing space.

Utilize planetary gear configuration for balanced load distribution and smooth motion.

Integrate with a 12V brushed DC motor for practical mechanical testing.

How?

Modeled in SolidWorks with custom gear geometry and 0.1 mm print tolerances for precision meshing.

Included 6 bearings, 3 planet gears, and 1 sun gear to achieve compact, stable operation.

Applied multi-material 3D printing and fastened components with 9 embedded screws for rigidity.

Results

Achieved 5× torque increase and 5× speed reduction from direct motor output.

Delivered 1 mm of precise motion at a 10 cm lever arm with minimal backlash.

Demonstrated an efficient, durable prototype suitable for small-scale robotic applications.

The Idea

Create a compact, lifelike robotic hand capable of mimicking human finger movement. Explore tendon-driven actuation for natural motion and smooth flexibility. Design a functional demonstration model built entirely within a weekend timeframe.

How?

Modeled all components in Onshape, refining finger geometry for strength and joint precision. Integrated 6 microservos and an Arduino Uno R3 for five programmable hand motions. Tested and iterated multiple elastic and tendon configurations for realistic finger motion and return tension.

Results

Improved finger strength by 40% through CAD optimization and material tuning. Achieved smooth, controllable actuation using a joystick interface. Completed a fully functional prototype despite material limits, time constraints, and compact design challenges.

The Idea

Build an ultra-small, affordable robot dog after finding existing models too expensive. Overcome the power limitations of microservos by distributing load through clever mechanical design. Use elastic support systems to assist servo torque and mimic muscle-like movement.

How?

Designed and prototyped a single robotic leg as a proof of concept. Implemented dual diagonal servo actuation with elastic tension sharing the upward load. Tested lifting performance with 3D-printed components and lightweight linkages for structural efficiency.

Results

Prototype leg successfully lifted 500 g, proving feasibility at small scale. Demonstrated that microservos can achieve realistic motion when aided by elastic support. Project halted before full quadruped completion due to electronics cost and control complexity, but validated key design principles.

The Idea

Create a high-efficiency hydro generator by eliminating bearing friction through magnetic levitation. Use overengineering as exploration, testing the limits of unconventional generator design. Incorporate a flywheel system to maintain smooth, consistent rotation during operation.

How?

Built the frame from 2020 aluminum extrusions for precise alignment and rigidity. Integrated 60 N52 neodymium magnets for levitation and power generation. Wound custom 20 AWG copper coils and coupled them with a high-inertia flywheel for steady energy output.

Results

Generated a peak power output of 6 W with an overall efficiency of ~5%. Found that magnetic interference between the levitation and generator systems caused major energy losses. Despite inefficiency, the project successfully demonstrated magnetic levitation feasibility in a functional generator setup.

The Idea

Create a scale 3D-printed model of the Ferrari 335s. Use the project as an opportunity to learn advanced surfacing and design intent in Onshape. Reproduce the cars complex, flowing curves as accurately as possible within one month.

How?

Used reference geometry and sketches to match the car’s proportions and curvature. Applied parametric modeling techniques to allow for smooth iteration and refinement. Conducted multiple design revisions to achieve realistic body contours and accurate details.

Results

Produced a highly accurate 3D model closely resembling the Ferrari 335s. Learned advanced CAD surfacing and curvature control through hands-on experimentation. Final design achieved strong visual realism, with only minor imperfections beyond project scope.
